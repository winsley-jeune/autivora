# Autivara Keyword & Content Strategy — Demand-First

## The correction
Our early content mirrored competitors ("Pura vs Drift," "Aroma360 alternatives"). Two problems: (1) competitors already own those SERPs, and (2) it only catches people *already comparing brands* — a tiny bottom slice. The real volume in this niche is **problem queries** ("why does my car smell") and **aspiration queries** ("how to make my house smell like a hotel"). Validated by research below — those SERPs are packed with competitor articles, but almost none are from diffuser brands (they're detailers, cleaning sites, publishers). **That's our opening.**

> **On volume numbers:** I will NOT invent monthly-search-volume figures. Demand bands below (High/Med/Low) are inferred from *how much competing content ranks* + query breadth — a real signal. Before locking the calendar, validate exact MSV in a free tool: **Google Keyword Planner**, **Ahrefs Free Keyword Generator**, **AnswerThePublic**, or just **Google autocomplete + "People also ask"**.

## The 5 demand clusters (replace the competitor-mirror approach)

### 🅐 "Smell like [place / brand]" — HERO cluster
**Why:** highest commercial intent in the niche — the searcher wants a *specific scent outcome*, which is exactly an oil sale. Big head terms are competitive, but the **per-brand long-tail is wide open and converts.**
**Demand:** High. **Difficulty:** Mixed (head hard, long-tail easy). **Attach:** oils/`scents` + diffuser as delivery.

Real query patterns:
- `how to make my house smell like a hotel` · `what scent do hotels use` · `hotel lobby scent`
- `how to make my car smell like a new car` / `like a luxury car`
- `what does [Lululemon / Aritzia / Abercrombie] store smell like` · `[brand] store scent dupe`
- Named hotel scents: `Westin White Tea scent` · `1 Hotels scent` · `Marriott lobby scent`
- `make my house smell like [Anthropologie / Capri Blue Volcano]`

> ⚠️ **Trademark caution:** never claim to *be* or *replicate* a brand's fragrance. Use "**inspired by**," "**reminiscent of**," "**capture the feeling of**." (Fragrance-dupe trademark disputes are real.)

### 🅑 "Why does my [space] smell [bad/musty/like X]" — problem cluster
**Why:** huge problem-aware demand. Detailers & cleaning sites own the *removal* answer — we win by owning the **"…and how to keep it smelling good"** follow-through (which is where the diffuser goes).
**Demand:** High. **Difficulty:** Med. **Attach:** car + home diffusers.

Real query patterns:
- `why does my car smell like mildew / gas / smoke / musty` · `how to get [smell] out of car`
- `why does my room / bedroom smell musty` · `why does my bedroom smell in the morning`
- `why does my apartment / basement / closet smell musty`

### 🅒 "How to make [place] smell good" — broad how-to cluster
**Why:** the widest top-of-funnel volume; every space is its own keyword.
**Demand:** High. **Difficulty:** Med. **Attach:** the matching per-space diffuser.

Real query patterns:
- `how to make my room / bedroom smell good` (and *...all the time / naturally*)
- `how to make my house smell good` · `how to make bathroom / apartment / office / closet smell good`
- `how to make my car smell good` *(we have this — it's the template for the rest)*

### 🅓 Diffuser blends & oil recipes — LTV cluster
**Why:** directly drives **oil/refill** sales (your margin engine) and it's evergreen + endlessly repeatable.
**Demand:** High. **Difficulty:** Med (oil sellers compete, but seasonal/use-case long-tail is open). **Attach:** `scents` oils + diffusers.

Real query patterns:
- `essential oil diffuser blends for sleep / focus / energy / anxiety / stress`
- `diffuser recipes for fall / winter / Christmas / spring`
- `best essential oils for sleep / cleaning / mood / a cold`
- `how many drops of oil in a diffuser` · `diffuser blend ratios`

### 🅔 "What scent does [business type] use" — B2B-aligned cluster
**Why:** pairs with the B2B channel; lower volume, higher value, and it feeds commercial leads.
**Demand:** Med. **Difficulty:** Low–Med. **Attach:** Atmos commercial units + `B2B_OUTREACH_KIT.md`.

Real query patterns:
- `what scent do hotels / gyms / salons / spas use`
- `how to make my Airbnb smell good` · `what do Airbnb hosts use for scent`
- `scent marketing for small business` · `how to scent a retail store`

### 🅕 Brand comparisons (Pura vs Drift, Aroma360 alt) — MAINTAIN, don't expand
Keep the existing set live (it catches bottom-funnel comparison traffic), but **stop adding to it.** It's competitor-owned and narrow. Redirect effort to A–E.

**Then scale the long-tail:** one article per space (🅒: living room, apartment, closet, office, kitchen, whole house), per problem (🅑: smoke, pet, gas, basement), per season/use-case blend (🅓), and per brand/hotel "inspired by" (🅐 — the deepest, most convertible vein).

---

## How each piece must convert (don't repeat the old mistake)
Every article: (1) answer the query genuinely first (earns the ranking + trust), (2) attach the **right product** at the decision point (problem → diffuser; "smell like X" → an oil profile; blend → oils), (3) internally link to the relevant **pillar guide** and `scents`. Top-of-funnel traffic is worthless if it doesn't route to a product.
