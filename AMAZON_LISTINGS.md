## 1. Prerequisites & Setup Checklist

| Item | Recommendation for Autivara |
|---|---|
| **Seller plan** | Start on the **Professional plan ($39.99/mo)**, not Individual. Individual charges $0.99/sale and locks you out of advertising (PPC), the Buy Box, bulk tools, and Brand Registry — all of which you need at launch. The flat fee pays for itself above ~40 units/mo. |
| **GTIN / UPC** | Amazon requires a **GTIN (UPC/EAN)** to create most listings. Buy genuine UPCs from **GS1 (gs1.org)** — avoid cheap resold barcodes, which Amazon increasingly rejects. Budget one UPC per SKU **per variation** (each color/scent child needs its own). |
| **GTIN exemption** | Once you have **Brand Registry** (below), you can apply for a **GTIN exemption** and list under your brand without buying UPCs. Worth doing after the trademark clears; until then, use GS1 UPCs. |
| **Brand Registry + trademark** | File a **USPTO word mark for "Autivara"** (or use an active application via Amazon's **IP Accelerator**). Brand Registry unlocks A+ Content, Brand Store, Sponsored Brands ads, Vine, and listing-hijack protection. This is the single highest-leverage setup step for a premium brand. |
| **FBA vs FBM** | **Launch FBM (self-fulfilled) for the high-ticket commercial/home units** to preserve cash and avoid storage fees on slow movers. **Use FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) for the giftable sub-$50 hero SKUs** — Prime badge + Buy Box dramatically lifts conversion, and these are small/light so FBA fees stay reasonable. Hybrid is fine. |
| **Images** | **Main image: product only on a pure white background (RGB 255/255/255), product filling ~85% of frame, no text/logos/props.** Then 6–8 secondary images: lifestyle (in-car / on-desk), scale, "how it works," refill/"own your scent," dimensions infographic, and a packaging/gift shot. 1600px+ on the long side to enable zoom. |
| **Listing prep** | Have ready per SKU: title, 5 bullets, description, backend search terms, A+ modules, and **accurate** dimensions/weight (for FBA fee + shipping calc). |

## 5. Amazon Pricing Note (Protect Margin)

**The math that matters:** Amazon takes a **~15% referral fee** on most Home & Automotive categories, plus **FBA fulfillment fees** (roughly a few dollars per small/light unit) and possible storage. On a sub-$50 product, referral + FBA can absorb **25–40%+** of the sale price before COGS. Do not race competitors to the bottom — Autivara's value is design and "own your scent," not lowest price.

| SKU | DTC | Recommended Amazon action |
|---|---|---|
| Solar $29 | $29 | **Bump to ~$34–$39 on Amazon.** At $29, FBA fees gut the margin; the higher price still reads as a giftable impulse buy. Or sell **FBM** to dodge FBA fees. |
| Cabin $39 | $39 | **Hold or nudge to $39–$44.** Works on Amazon; healthy enough to absorb fees, especially via FBA Prime lift. |
| Ember / Nimbus / Nova $44 | $44 | **Hold at $44 (consider $45–$49).** The sweet spot — premium enough to absorb fees and stay profitable. Lead SKUs. |
| Phase-2 $24 SKUs (Astronaut, Aviator, Riff, Orchard) | $24 | **DTC-first, or bundle on Amazon.** At $24 the Amazon math is brutal solo. Sell as **2-packs / gift bundles** to clear the fee hurdle, or keep DTC-only. |
| Atmos Wi-Fi $149 / Pro $349 / Plug $79 | — | **Keep DTC (or Amazon Business / FBM).** High-ticket commercial units convert best with direct sales support and consultative content; Amazon's referral fee on $349 is a large flat hit with little upside vs. your own site. |

**Price parity:** Amazon's pricing policy discourages listing **higher** on Amazon than you advertise elsewhere. Practical approach: set Amazon at a **modest premium that you mirror on the DTC site** (e.g., raise Solar to ~$34–$39 everywhere), and reserve true discounts for **DTC-exclusive bundles, subscriptions, and email coupons** so your owned channel always offers the best total value without violating parity.

## 6. Launch Sequence — First 30 Days

**Reviews (honest only — no fake/incentivized reviews; it's against policy and risks account suspension):**
- **Enroll Brand-Registered SKUs in Amazon Vine** (you supply free units; Vine reviewers leave verified, unbiased reviews). This is the compliant way to seed your first reviews. Prioritize the 5 hero SKUs.
- Use the **"Request a Review"** button (or Brand-tailored review automation) on every order — neutral, Amazon-sanctioned outreach.
- Drive your **existing DTC customers / email list** to buy on Amazon and review honestly. Insert a polite "enjoying it? a review helps" card — never gate it on incentives.

**Sales velocity (honest):**
- Point **external traffic** (your email list, Instagram, TikTok organic) to the Amazon listings in the first 2 weeks — early velocity teaches the algorithm to rank you.
- Use the **Amazon Attribution** tags so off-site traffic is tracked; consider a small **launch coupon** (clip-on % off) to lift conversion without permanently lowering price.
- Make sure all 7+ images, A+ Content, and the Brand Store are **live before** you send traffic — conversion drives ranking.

**PPC starter:**
- Begin with **Sponsored Products – Auto campaigns** on each hero SKU for the first ~2 weeks to harvest real converting search terms; cap a modest daily budget per SKU.
- Then split into **manual exact/phrase campaigns** on the winners (e.g., "volcano diffuser," "jellyfish diffuser," "rechargeable car diffuser"), and **negative-match** the junk terms.
- Add a **Sponsored Brands** headline ad (requires Brand Registry) once 2–3 SKUs are live to drive traffic to the Brand Store.

**30-day cadence (quick view):**

| Days | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1–7 | Listings + A+ + images live · Vine enrollment · auto-PPC on |
| 8–14 | External/email traffic to listings · launch coupon · "Request a Review" running |
| 15–21 | Harvest PPC search terms → build manual campaigns · negative keywords |
| 22–30 | Double down on converting keywords · add Sponsored Brands · evaluate which sub-$50 SKUs justify staying on Amazon vs. DTC |
